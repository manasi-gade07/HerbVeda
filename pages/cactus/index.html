Cactus

Cactus is known for its ability to survive in harsh, dry environments.Cactus plants are drought-tolerant succulents, popular for their unique appearance and ability to store water.

Region: Arid

Common Names: English: Cactus Spanish: Cacto Hindi: Nagphani

Type: Succulent

Habitat: Found in deserts and dry, rocky areas with minimal rainfall. Adapted to hot climates with poor soils.

Botanical Name: Cactaceae family (Multiple genera, including Opuntia and Cereus)

Medicinal Uses: Skin Care: Some cactus extracts are used for treating sunburn and wounds. Hydration: The juice of certain cacti, like prickly pear, is used to rehydrate the body. Anti-inflammatory: Helps reduce inflammation. Digestive Aid: Promotes digestion and regulates blood sugar levels. Antioxidants: Rich in compounds that fight oxidative stress.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Requires hot, dry climates with minimal humidity. Tolerates temperatures up to 40°C. Soil Requirements: Needs sandy, well-drained soil. Prefers slightly alkaline soils. Propagation: Propagated through cuttings or seeds. Cuttings must be left to dry for a few days before planting. Irrigation: Water sparingly every 2-4 weeks. Avoid waterlogging, which can rot the roots. Pest and Disease Control: Rarely affected by pests, but watch for mealybugs. Harvesting: Fruits, like prickly pears, are harvested when ripe. Pads are also harvested for food or medicinal use.
