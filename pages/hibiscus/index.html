Hibiscus

Hibiscus is a tropical flowering plant known for its vibrant flowers and health benefits.

Region: Tropical

Common Names: English: Hibiscus Hindi: Gudhal Spanish: Hibisco

Type: Flowering Plant

Habitat: Grows in gardens, wetlands, and coastal areas. Prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Botanical Name: Hibiscus sabdariffa (for tea) Hibiscus rosa-sinensis (ornamental)

Medicinal Uses: Blood Pressure: Known to help lower blood pressure. Antioxidant: Rich in antioxidants, promoting overall health. Digestive Aid: Supports digestive health and may act as a mild laxative. Anti-inflammatory: Reduces inflammation and may help with skin issues.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunlight. Soil Requirements: Prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Tolerates slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. Propagation: Can be propagated from seeds or cuttings. Irrigation: Requires regular watering, especially during dry periods. Pest and Disease Control: Prone to aphids and whiteflies. Use organic insecticides if necessary. Harvesting: Flowers are harvested for tea and other uses, usually in the morning.
