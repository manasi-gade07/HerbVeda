Lavender

Lavender is a fragrant herb known for its calming properties and use in aromathera.

Region: Mediterranean

Common Names: English: Lavender Hindi: Lavandula Spanish: Lavanda

Type: Flowering Plant

Habitat: Grows in well-drained, sandy soils. Prefers sunny locations with low humidity.

Botanical Name: Lavandula angustifolia

Medicinal Uses: Relaxation: Used for stress relief and promoting sleep. Antiseptic: Has mild antiseptic properties, useful for minor cuts and burns. Anxiety Relief: Helps reduce anxiety and improve mood. Digestive Aid: May help alleviate digestive issues.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Prefers warm, dry climates with full sunlight. Soil Requirements: Thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Propagation: Propagated from cuttings or seeds. Irrigation: Requires minimal watering; drought-tolerant once established. Pest and Disease Control: Resistant to most pests; watch for spider mites. Harvesting: Flowers are harvested just before fully open for best aroma.
