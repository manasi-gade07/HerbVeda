Rosemary

HRosemary is a fragrant herb known for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Region: Mediterranean

Common Names: English: Rosemary Hindi: Rozmarin Spanish: Romero

Type: Herb

Habitat: Prefers sunny locations with well-drained soil. Thrives in Mediterranean climates.

Botanical Name: Rosmarinus officinalis

Medicinal Uses: Memory Enhancement: Traditionally used to improve memory and concentration. Digestive Aid: Helps with digestion and may relieve bloating. Antioxidant: Rich in antioxidants, promoting overall health. Anti-inflammatory: Reduces inflammation and pain.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Grows best in warm, sunny climates. Soil Requirements: Prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Propagation: Can be propagated through cuttings or seeds. Irrigation: Requires minimal watering; drought-tolerant once established. Pest and Disease Control: Generally pest-resistant; watch for aphids. Harvesting: Leaves can be harvested as needed; best before flowering.
