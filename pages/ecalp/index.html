Eucalyptus

Eucalyptus is a fast-growing evergreen tree known for its medicinal properties, especially for respiratory health. It is also widely cultivated for timber and essential oils.

Region: Temperate

Common Names: English: Eucalyptus Hindi: Nilgiri Tamil: Neelamaram Telugu: Neelagiri Chettu

Type: Tree

Habitat: Found in dry forests, woodlands, and grasslands. Grows well in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soils.

Botanical Name: Eucalyptus globulus

Medicinal Uses: Respiratory Health: Eucalyptus oil is used to relieve coughs, colds, and sinus congestion. Anti-inflammatory: Reduces pain and swelling in joints and muscles. Antiseptic: Used in wound care for its antibacterial properties. Mental Alertness: Inhalation of eucalyptus oil helps improve focus and relieve fatigue. Insect Repellent: Used to repel mosquitoes and other insects.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Prefers temperatures between 15°C to 35°C. Requires full sunlight and can withstand drought conditions. Soil Requirements: Thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils. Can grow in nutrient-poor soils with minimal care. Propagation: Propagated through seeds or cuttings. Seeds are sown in nurseries before transplanting. Irrigation: Requires minimal water. Water once every 10-15 days during dry seasons. Overwatering may cause root rot. Pest and Disease Control: Susceptible to termites and fungal infections. Use organic pesticides if necessary. Harvesting: Leaves are harvested 4-5 months after planting to extract essential oils.

ExtractionProcess: Essential oil is toxic if ingested; may cause skin irritation.
