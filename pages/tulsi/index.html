Tulsi

Tulsi is a sacred herb in Hinduism, known for its medicinal properties and spiritual significance.

Region: Tropical

Common Names: English: Holy Basil Hindi: Tulsi Spanish: Albahaca sagrada

Type: Herb

Habitat: Grows well in gardens and backyards. Prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Botanical Name: Ocimum sanctum or Ocimum tenuiflorum

Medicinal Uses: Stress Relief: Known for its adaptogenic properties, reducing stress and anxiety. Immune Booster: Enhances immune function and overall health. Anti-inflammatory: Reduces inflammation and pain. Respiratory Health: Supports respiratory function and alleviates coughs.

Methods of Cultivation: Climate Requirements: Prefers warm, tropical climates. Soil Requirements: Thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Propagation: Propagated through seeds or cuttings. Irrigation: Requires regular watering; keep soil consistently moist. Pest and Disease Control: Susceptible to aphids and whiteflies; use organic pesticides if necessary. Harvesting: Leaves can be harvested as needed, best used fresh for medicinal purposes.
